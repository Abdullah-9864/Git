6 Types of Financial Statement Analysis
1. Horizontal Analysis
2. Vertical Analysis
3. Trend Analysis
4. Ratio Analysis
5. Comparative Analysis
6. Cash Flow Analysis
1
Horizontal Analysis
Compare same company across MULTIPLE YEARS — also called Time Series Analysis or Trend % Analysis
Take one year as BASE YEAR and compare all other years against it. Shows growth or decline over time.
Change % = (Current Year − Base Year) ÷ Base Year × 100
Item2022 (Base)2023Change %2024Change %
Revenue400,000450,000+12.5%500,000+25%
Net Profit80,00090,000+12.5%120,000+50%
Total Assets600,000620,000+3.3%700,000+16.7%
Reading: Revenue grew 25% in 2 years. Profit grew faster (50%) — efficiency improved!
✓ Best for: Spotting growth trends, identifying problem years, planning future targets
2
Vertical Analysis
Express every item as % of a BASE FIGURE — also called Common Size Analysis
For Income Statement: every item as % of Revenue. For Balance Sheet: every item as % of Total Assets.
% = (Individual Item ÷ Base Figure) × 100

✓ Best for: Comparing companies of different sizes, seeing cost structure, easy comparison
3
Trend Analysis
Look at patterns over LONG PERIOD (5-10 years) to predict future direction
Similar to horizontal but focuses on the DIRECTION and PATTERN rather than just year to year change. Used to forecast future performance.
Trend % = (Current Year Value ÷ Base Year Value) × 100
Base year is always = 100

4
Ratio Analysis
Calculate mathematical relationships between financial figures — most widely used type
Has 5 sub-categories. Each answers a different question about the company.
Liquidity Ratios
Can company pay SHORT TERM debts?
• Current Ratio
• Quick Ratio (Acid Test)
• Cash Ratio
• Net Working Capital
Leverage / Debt Ratios
How much DEBT does company have?
• Debt to Equity
• Debt Ratio
• Interest Coverage
• Equity Multiplier
Activity / Efficiency Ratios
How well are ASSETS being used?
• Inventory Turnover
• Receivables Turnover
• Asset Turnover
• Avg Collection Period
Profitability Ratios
Is company making PROFIT?
• Gross Profit Margin
• Net Profit Margin
• ROA, ROE, ROI
• EBIT Margin
Market / Valuation Ratios
Is the STOCK worth buying?  • EPS  • P/E Ratio  • Dividend Yield  • Price to Book
✓ Best for: Quick health check, comparing with industry standards, identifying weak areas
5
Comparative Analysis
Compare your company against COMPETITORS or INDUSTRY AVERAGE
Also called Cross-Sectional Analysis or Benchmarking. Tells you how your company performs relative to others in the same industry.

6
Cash Flow Analysis
Analyze actual CASH MOVEMENTS — profit on paper vs real cash in hand
A company can show profit on income statement but still run out of cash! Cash flow analysis checks if real cash is available for operations, investment, and debt repayment.
Cash flow has 3 sections:
Operating Activities — cash from day to day business (selling goods)
Investing Activities — cash from buying/selling assets (machinery, buildings)
Financing Activities — cash from loans, issuing shares, paying dividends
